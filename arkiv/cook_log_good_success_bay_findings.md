### Remarks Section - Jan 15, 1769 (first day anchored)

Header: "Remarks &c in Good Success Bay — Terra del Fuego"

**Jan 15 (SSE wind):**
"Fresh breeze and squally weather with some showers of rain — at 2 PM standing into the bay depth of water from 14 to 10 & 9 fath. sandy ground — At ½ past came too with ½ best Bow.s in 9 fath. veer'd away to 1½ a cable and moor'd ship (with??) stream anchor and cable to y N..."

"Hoisted out the boats and went the Pinnace (with a Lieut?) to look for a watering place — saw [Van?] dians ashore 2 of whom came off in the boat after she had found a convenient place to wood & water; they were of middling stature, and wore a [shid?] over their bodies. Buf and Bread they were very fond of and indeed every thing given them except Spirituous liquors — at 6 vessels boat ashore with em."

"People employ'd preparing empty casks &c for watering and wooding."

**Bearings at anchorage (Jan 15):**
"When moor'd the Rock to ¾ W of the Sandy Beach N 70 W — the other end of the beach N 35 W — watering place S 10 W — The Elm[?] of the bay S 80 E — the M[?] N 18 E — Ext of Staten land from Cape Bartholomew N 85 E to N 61°8 Lea."

**Jan 15-16 (W°):**
"Mr Banks, Dr Solander, Mr Green &c went up into Country — A.M. a Lieut with a party of Seamen and Marines went ashore to fill water and cut wood — A Boat surveying the..."

**Jan 16 (W°):**
"Foot and middle parts moderate breeze with some squalls of hail and rain and at times great falls of Snow — rec'd on board [???] Sons of some wood; employ'd stowing it away — build wild celery in..."

### Continuation Jan 17-20 (bottom section of image 57)

**Jan 17-18 (partially visible):**
Continuing woodcutting and watering operations

**Jan 19 (JW):**
"Moderate gales and Cloudy weather. PM Employ'd on board as yesterday — The Lieut with the wooders and waterers landed, as did the Boatswain with a party to cut brooms — The Hawser, which was bent to the kedge anchor to ride the longboat by, whilst her People hauld the casks of water off from the surf [?]; two boats were sent to sweep for the anchor, it could not be found — Stowing away on board — Weigh'd stream anchor."

**Jan 20 (heading "20 ... From Good Success Bay towards Port royal Bay"):**
Wind: WJW — "Moderate and cloudy with some flying showers of rain"

## Assessment for Longitude Research

The Good Success Bay entries (image 57) do **NOT** appear to contain an explicit longitude observation by direct astronomical measurement. Instead:

1. Cook records the **observed latitude as 55°22'S** on Jan 20 (departure day)
2. Longitude was likely determined by dead reckoning from the known position of Rio de Janeiro
3. The bearings of landmarks at anchorage (N 70 W to sandy beach, etc.) are recorded but these are relative compass bearings, not absolute longitude
4. The log does not mention "Greenwich," "Meridian," or lunar distance observations during the Good Success Bay stay

**Note:** The actual longitude observations may be in Cook's separate astronomical journal or in Green's observations. The ship's log was primarily for navigation and daily events — precise longitude from lunar distances would typically be in a separate record.
