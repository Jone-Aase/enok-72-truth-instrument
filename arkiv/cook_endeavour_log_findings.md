### What Cook Wrote In His Own Hand:

**Header**: "H K F Courses Winds h 21 January 1769 .... From Good Success Bay"

**January 21, 1769 entries include**:
- "fresh gales and squally with rain"
- "set Mizetops."
- "mod.te and Cloudy – stow'd the best Bow.t anchor"
- "Light airs and Cloudy with rain"
- "Out 2 reefs and sway'd up Topg.t yds."
- "unbent y.e Cable and stow'd the small Bower"
- "out all reefs"
- "tack'd ship { In 1 reef Maintops.l and 1 and 2 Foretops.l / Fresh Gales and squally weather"

**January 22, 1769 (♀ 22) entries include**:
- "moderate and Cloudy – Out 2reefs Foretops.l" (distance: Ⓗ22)
- "Squally with Fließing showers"
- "Sounded ... No ground at 200 fath."
- "D.o weat.r Down Topg.t yds."
- "mod.te and Cloudy"
- "Saw the land to the SW... tack'd ship"
- "tack'd ship ... An island WSW"
- Calm: "The Extr.s of the land a NNE 10 or 11 Leag to SWBW 9 or 10. A remarkable round bluff head WNW 5 or 6 – 2 small islands SW 6 or 7–"
- "Little wind and Cloudy – Sway'd up Topg.t yds and out all Reefs set Steering sails"

**January 23, 1769 (♃ 23) entries include**:
- "Moderate and Cloudy with rain" (distance: Ⓗ23)
- "Extr.s of the land a N.to SW"
- "In 1 reef Tops.ls the land NWBW a small island WBS / Cloudy. Variation per med. of azimuths ... 21.47 E"
- "Land squal"
- "Little wind and cloudy"
- "taken aback"
- "Rain"
- "fresh gales and squally"
- "Saw the land to the W.t making in sev.l Islands"
- "A small sugar loaf island NWBW – Extr.s of the islands a WWBW to NW"
- "Sounded 40 fath. – Sand and broken shells"

### What Cook Wrote In His Own Hand:

**Header**: "H K F Courses Winds ♀ 24 ..... towards Port Royal Bay"

**January 24, 1769 entries**:
- "Fresh gales and squally with rain"
- "In 2 reefs Tops."
- "moderate and cloudy ~ East of the land a N to S.36 W"
- "tack'd ship ~ The Point of the small island is WNW 3 or 4 m. and the 2 Sm.t NBW"
- "In past [?] Cabo in sight SWBS 5 Leag — [a low p.t] **Cape Horn** SWBS 5 Lea..."
- "moderate and cloudy with hail and rain"
- "out 2 reefs Fortops."
- "saw the land to y W.t **Variation per Amplitude .......... 21.16 E.**"
- "light wind and hazy"
- "**Cape Horn** S.33°.W. 5 or 6 Leag."
- "Saw a remarkable peak SW"
- "Fresh gales and squally with small rain"
- "The p.t yet last Sight for **Cape Horn** WBN. 2 Leag. — **This Point is the SE extremity of two islands; near it bye some pointed Craggy rocks**"
- "Sounded 35 fath... round stones and broken shells"
- Distance: 34.6

**January 25, 1769 (♀ 25) entries**:
- "Fresh gales and squally – In 1st 2d reefs Tops. – at h. past **Cape Horn** NNW a pointed island to y.t S.t W.t 0 or Leag. ♀ 25"
- "out 2 reefs Tops."
- "moderate"
- "thick hazy weat. with small rain – passed a rippling"
- "Thick Fog"
- Distance: 74

**January 26, 1769 (♀ 26) entries**:
- "moderate breezes and Cloudy.... A SW Swell" (♀ 26)
- Multiple tacking entries
- "Fresh gales... Very large Albatrosses at the ship"

## Longitude Information

The specific longitude of **68°13'W** for Cape Horn mentioned by Cook is recorded in the journal entries (not the ship's log). The ship's log records "Longitude made" (relative) not absolute longitude. The absolute longitude would be calculated from chronometer or lunar distance observations.

The Diego Ramirez Islands (visible from this position) are at approximately 68°43'W, consistent with a Cape Horn position of ~68°13'W.
